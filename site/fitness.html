Built on Scientific Research

Every program is backed by peer-reviewed studies and exercise science

01

Progressive Overload

Systematically increase training stress through volume, intensity, or frequency. Studies show 5-10% weekly load increases optimize adaptation while minimizing injury risk.

Research: Schoenfeld et al. (2017) - Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research
02

Stimulus-Recovery-Adaptation

Training creates stimulus, recovery allows adaptation. Muscle protein synthesis peaks 24-48 hours post-training. Adequate rest is crucial for hypertrophy.

Research: Damas et al. (2016) - Sports Medicine
03

Specificity & Variation

Train specific to your goals (SAID principle) while varying exercises to prevent adaptation plateau. Research shows varied training increases strength by 2-3% more than monotonous protocols.

Research: Fonseca et al. (2014) - Sports Medicine
04

Periodization

Structured training phases optimize performance. Linear, undulating, and block periodization all show superior results to non-periodized training (+15-20% strength gains).

Research: Williams et al. (2017) - Sports Medicine
